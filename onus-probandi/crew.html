Key Creative Team

Onus Probandi brings together experienced independent filmmakers with a strong track record in character-driven storytelling and award-winning short form production.

Writer

Chris Beadnell

Writer – Onus Probandi

Chris Beadnell is an award-winning Australian filmmaker specialising in emotionally driven narratives across thriller, drama, mystery, and dark comedy genres. His screenwriting work has received more than 75 film festival awards across multiple short films and screenplays, demonstrating strong international recognition.

His original short screenplay Onus Probandi has itself received multiple awards and festival placements, reflecting its appeal across both narrative and screenplay competitions. Chris previously collaborated with director Adrian Alback on the short film Cliff’s Edge (2023), which achieved 15 award wins on the festival circuit.

In 2024, his short film The Note won Best Screenplay at the International Film Festival Australia (IFFA) and Best Male Director at the Power24 International Film Festival in Durban. His 2025 feature adaptation of Bingo Man received Best Feature Screenplay at the Melbourne International Film Festival.

Chris specialises in character-driven storytelling that explores emotional vulnerability within suspenseful narrative frameworks, often working within micro-budget production environments while maximising creative impact.

Producer / Director

Adrian Alback

Producer / Director – Onus Probandi

Adrian Alback is an Australian broadcast and online production specialist with extensive experience across live television, livestreaming, and independent film production. His previous directing credit Cliff’s Edge (2023) received 15 international festival awards.

Adrian’s background in broadcast audio engineering and multi-camera production includes roles with Reuters Television (Tokyo), Channel Nine Brisbane, Global Television Sydney, and Channel Seven Brisbane. He holds AFTRS AD certification and specialises in multi-platform production workflows bridging broadcast and digital delivery.

Current projects include the feature documentary Blue Hermit (2026) and the technology-focused documentary Let’s Not Google Meet.
