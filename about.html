About

Webstreamedia

Webstreamedia is a Brisbane-based Australian production company, registered as Webstreamedia Pty Ltd, ABN 38 152 737 872, operating from Griffin in Brisbane’s outer north.

The company’s principal is Adrian Alback, a Queensland-based director, producer and senior broadcast professional whose three-and-a-half decade career across Australian and international television gives Webstreamedia an unusually deep technical and musical foundation for the development of long-form film and documentary.

Background

Adrian’s foundational training is in audio engineering, completed at the Queensland Conservatorium of Music in 1987. His first hands-on film work was in London in 1991, at a post-production facility working on sound dubbing across 16mm and 35mm features and serials.

Returning to Australia later that year, he was engaged as sound recordist on a series of low-budget Australian features, and in 1997 was recruited from full-time work at Channel Seven to work on the Marque Owen-directed 35mm theatrical feature All The Way (released 1998, distributed by Beyond).

Across the subsequent decade, Adrian held senior audio direction positions at Seven Network Sydney (audio-directing Sunrise, 11AM, Witness and Australia’s Most Wanted) and Global Television Sydney (national sport and variety productions), and worked as Audio Director on NBC Sports’ Paris-based outside broadcast of the 1991 French Open. His Channel Nine Brisbane career (1989–1992) included credits on A Current Affair, Wide World of Sports, Today and Sixty Minutes.

Directing and producing

Adrian’s directing and producing work runs alongside the broadcast career. He produced and directed the 25-minute documentary Atom Heart Anatomy in 2009, took an associate producer credit on the mid-length drama To Face the Sun (45 min, 2011), and directed health-sector and educational projects through the 2010s.

From 2023, his independent directing work has accelerated: the short film Cliff’s Edge (2023, writer: Chris Beadnell), the feature documentary Blue Hermit currently in production about jazz composer Dr Jeff Usher, and the short crime mystery Onus Probandi (writer: Chris Beadnell) currently shooting.

Current focus

Webstreamedia’s current strategic focus is the development of long-form Australian content for theatrical, streaming and broadcast release, including a feature drama project currently in confidential development.

The company also continues to provide senior broadcast services and consultation to selected Queensland and national clients.
